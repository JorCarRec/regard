SCP-8213

Item #: SCP-8213

Object Class: Keter  Apollyon 

Special Containment Procedures: SCP-8213 is held within  an unlit vertical shaft  beneath Site-19,  Sublevel  ███. The shaft is to remain unlit at all times. It is not to be watched. It is not to be filmed. It is not to be  rendered or described , and it is not to be  approached . No personnel are to look into  the shaft , at recovered  footage or stills , or at this document past  the clearance acknowledgement . This file is an observation node. Reading it  is itself an act of observation . [REDACTED]

Under no circumstances is SCP-8213 to be  observed .

Description: SCP-8213 is a  humanoid  figure,  2.1  m in height, recovered from  a sealed geode at Sublevel 34  in  2015 . Under continuous observation it does not move. The instant it is not observed, it advances toward  the observer  — always toward whoever is  watching it , always by the shortest  path . Its rate of ascent  scales with the number of eyes currently upon it  [DATA EXPUNGED].

No observation of SCP-8213 has ever been  survived, reversed, or contained . Personnel who looked  set hard, like the aggregate,  within  72  hours and  could not be recovered . SCP-8213 is composed of  every person who has ever looked at it , and it is  not finished .

§1 Special Containment

Containment of SCP-8213 relies on complete information quarantine. Physical barriers cannot resist its displacement. Containment is achieved solely by eliminating every direct and electronic vector of observation.

SCP-8213 is designated to an unlit deep-storage utility shaft at Site-19 ("The Well"). Under Protocol 8213-DARKOUT, all optical cameras, acoustic pickups, thermal sensors, and data relays within the shaft are permanently severed. The shaft cap at Sublevel 1 is reinforced with a 1.2-meter pour of high-density structural concrete. Personnel may not deploy inspection devices or lower-shaft hardware without unanimous approval from the Site-19 Containment Committee and the O5 Council.

Physical inspections are delegated to Mobile Task Force Beta-7 ("Darkroom") under non-visual parameters. Bi-weekly tactile perimeter sweeps of the Sublevel 1 cap are executed without electrical equipment, illumination, or digital measurement devices. Any operative who directly perceives SCP-8213 by any means is to receive Class-A amnestic treatment and be reassigned from Site-19.

Directive 8213-Alpha — O5 Executive Order

Restoration of optical telemetry or environmental sensing within the shaft is forbidden. Displacement scales with the number of minds and machines resolving the entity's position: sustained routine observation moves it by centimeters, but concurrent mass-observation events have each produced single leaps of 30 to 90 meters toward the surface.

PERSONAL LOG · DR. HALDEN · LEAD CONTAINMENT, SITE-19SEALED

We are the only site on the register that contains something by promising never to look at it. New staff find that funny for about a week.

I have started leaving the lights on when I sleep. Not for me. I want something in this building to be looking at nothing, on purpose, and be fine.

§2 Description

SCP-8213 is a humanoid physical anomaly measuring 2.1 meters in height. It exhibits absolute kinetic stagnation (v = 0 m/s) during real-time observation by a conscious mind or a computational process. During unmonitored intervals it displaces toward the surface in less than one Planck interval.

Archival photographs taken before Protocol 8213-DARKOUT depict a hairless entity with slate-gray epidermal tissue and a ████████████████████. The surface lacks pores and muscular contraction markers and chemically resembles unrefined plaster with irregular curing fissures. SCP-8213 shows no respiration, ocular movement, thermal dissipation, electromagnetic emission, or ██████████████████. Frame-by-frame analysis of over 90,000 hours of archival footage confirms zero kinetic translation during monitored states.

The entity has never been recorded in the act of moving. Its position changes anyway, during the sub-second intervals between camera frames or shift transitions. The magnitude of each displacement scales directly with the number of independent human minds or automated network nodes processing data about the entity's precise location. Displacement is slow but reliable, and is always directed toward the largest concurrent concentration of observers.

Incident 8213-██ occurred when an unmonitored workstation ran a 60 fps loop of SCP-8213 across a 144 Hz display for 60 hours. Roughly ███████ render events per hour sustained an automated observation state with no observer present. Each event moved the entity by a fraction below the seismic alarm threshold; across sixty hours the displacement accrued to just over thirty meters before the loop was found. Nobody was watching the monitor. It did not matter.

The mechanism driving displacement is disputed. A working paper by Dr. ████████ (Department of Quantum Memetics) argues that SCP-8213 has no physical velocity. It behaves as an anti-entropic system whose baseline state sits at z = 0, the surface.

Department of Quantum Memetics — Working Paper 8213-09 (Excerpt)

"Distance along the shaft is an artifact of measurement. SCP-8213 does not climb. Each Cognitive Vector Event forces a partial collapse of its probability density function Ψ(z,t) toward the surface observer pool. Information processing does not push the entity. It forces it to materialize at a higher baseline density relative to the observers. 'SL-0' is not a floor. It is the moment there are enough of us looking that it is simply, everywhere, here."

§3 Discovery

SCP-8213 was recovered during deep-crust excavation for Site-19 Sublevel 34, sealed inside a geode whose formation at that depth has never been explained. The cavity's interior surface was ████████████████████████████. It was inert, resembled a statue, and was cataloged as one. It was classified Safe and held that classification for four years.

Routine survey cross-referencing ended that, revealing a vertical positional discrepancy of ███████ against the original excavation surveys with no work order and no personnel in between. Containment was escalated to active monitoring. The constant visual logging that followed accelerated the ascent. Confirmation that observation was the displacement vector was, in practice, answered by increasing observation.

Failed Containment & Termination AttemptsLEVEL 4

Attempt 8213-01 (Kinetic Ordnance): High-explosive charges detonated in the shaft during an unobserved interval did zero damage. Post-detonation inspection found the entity intact and higher, and the inspection team ████████████████████. It does not need to survive the attack. It only needs the attack to require someone to look.

Attempt 8213-02 (Mechanical Extraction): A crane assembly attempted relocation. Relocation required real-time camera tracking, and the resulting observation displaced the entity upward faster than the winches could retract.

Attempt 8213-03 (Concrete Barrier Pour): A 1.2 m plug was poured at Sublevel 1 while SCP-8213 was still deep in the shaft; the cure was monitored by camera and completed intact. When the entity later ascended into the pour, tactile and seismic inspection confirmed it had entered the concrete without cracking or shifting the aggregate. Its effective mass density drops to zero during a displacement, so the plug arrests nothing. It was completed and is maintained regardless.

8213-04 — Emergency Addendum: The SL-0 Projection

SCP-8213 reaching SL-0 does not produce a conventional surface breach. Under the Okonkwo model, the shaft position simply stops being meaningful. The entity ceases to hold a location and resolves at maximal probability density across the entire active observer pool at once.

In practical terms, every mind and every network node then holding SCP-8213's precise state becomes, simultaneously, a place the entity is. The Department of Quantum Memetics designates the projected result an XK-Class end-of-world scenario, distinct from the standard model in that it is not spatial. There is no front to hold and no perimeter to fall back to. There is only the count of who is looking.

Accordingly, the point of no return is defined not as a depth but as an observer threshold, Θ. Once the number of minds concurrently aware of SCP-8213's exact state exceeds Θ, containment becomes mathematically unrecoverable: reducing the count back below Θ would itself require confirming the count, and confirmation is observation. The system cannot be measured back into safety.

Standing contingency for a Θ-exceedance event is Protocol 8213-BLACKGLASS, the immediate and total severance of SCiPNET followed by site-wide Class-A amnestic dispersal. BLACKGLASS has never been carried to completion in exercise. Its terminal step, the simultaneous and permanent cessation of every active query on every terminal at once, has no tested mechanism and no proposed one.

All personnel accessing this record are reminded that the record is itself an active observer node. Reducing active nodes to zero is the only known mechanism to halt the displacement.
